AQI Reading List

Famous articles and official references related to AQI and air quality.

This page collects some of the most useful AQI explainers, global guideline pages, and air-pollution references so your website has a proper reading section as well.

Featured Sources

Trusted resources worth reading

These are widely referenced sources for AQI basics, health impact, global guideline thresholds, and air quality monitoring context.

WHO WHO Global Air Quality Guidelines

One of the most cited official references for pollutant guideline levels and the public-health meaning of cleaner air targets.

 US EPA Communicating Air Quality Conditions: The AQI

A strong AQI explainer that breaks down AQI categories, public communication, and what different AQI values indicate.

 WHO Ambient Air Pollution and Health

A commonly referenced fact sheet covering outdoor pollution, major health effects, and the global burden of exposure.

 US EPA AirData Basic Information

Useful for understanding how official air-quality data is organized, interpreted, and paired with AQI reporting.

 UNEP UNEP Air Pollution Resources

An international overview of air-pollution challenges, policy context, and why cleaner air matters beyond AQI dashboards alone.

 US EPA Particulate Matter (PM) Basics

A helpful companion article for understanding PM2.5 and PM10, which are often the strongest AQI drivers in many regions.

What To Read First

Best starting points for beginners

Start with the EPA AQI explainer if you want the fastest understanding of AQI bands and public meaning.

Read the WHO guideline page next if you want more serious health and threshold context.

Use the PM basics article when you want to understand why PM2.5 and PM10 often dominate AQI stories.
