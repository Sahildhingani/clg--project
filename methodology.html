Forecast Method

How the AQI dashboard turns pollutant averages into a simple 5-year outlook.

This page separates the explanation from the interaction layer, so visitors can understand the project logic without reading through every dashboard card first.

Step 1

Aggregate yearly pollutant averages

For each state and year, the dashboard averages district-level pollutant values for PM2.5, PM10, NO2, SO2, CO, and O3.

Step 2

Estimate AQI from the pollutant profile

A weighted AQI score is produced from those six pollutant averages. The score is then clamped into a 0-500 AQI range.

Step 3

Fit a simple linear trend

Each pollutant gets a browser-side linear regression model using the available historical years, and that trend is extended into 2026-2030.

AQI Bands

How the status labels are assigned

Good Moderate Poor Very Poor Severe

Higher AQI values indicate worse air quality and greater likely health impact. The explorer uses these bands consistently in the ranking list, forecast cards, and state summary badge.

Interpretation

What the forecast should and should not mean

The model is intentionally simple and transparent for demonstration purposes.

It extends recent pollutant direction rather than simulating weather, policy, or sudden industrial change.

It works best as a comparative classroom dashboard, not as an official regulatory forecast.

AQI Formula Shape

Weighted pollutant scoring

AQI = 1.25 × PM2.5 + 0.42 × PM10 + 0.8 × NO2 + 0.55 × SO2 + 24 × CO + 0.62 × O3

After generating future pollutant values with linear regression, the same AQI scoring method is used to convert each predicted pollutant profile into yearly AQI forecast cards.
